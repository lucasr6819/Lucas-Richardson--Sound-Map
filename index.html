Data Collection Methodology

Measurement Methods

The noise pollution data is collected through multiple validated sources:

  • Professional Monitoring: Calibrated equipment at fixed stations using standardized measurement types (dBA, DNL, NEF)
  • Government Stations: Official monitoring networks like ADM Soundscape and RSQA providing continuous 24/7 data
  • Academic Research: Environmental health studies from institutions like McGill University measuring neighborhood-level exposure
  • Citizen Science: Community monitoring stations with outdoor-mounted microphones providing local insights

Data Verification

Each data point is verified through multiple sources:

  • Official documentation from environmental agencies
  • Peer-reviewed academic research publications
  • Government regulatory documentation
  • Professional acoustic studies and impact assessments
  • Cross-referenced community feedback
